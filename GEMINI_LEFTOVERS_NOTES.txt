Database Schema & Defrost Logic (Developer Reference)

1) Product vs Inventory (relational separation)
  - Table: `product_master` (global product definitions)
    - `id` (PK)
    - `name` (string)
    - `category_id` (FK -> Categories)
    - `default_fridge_life` (int: days)
    - `default_freezer_life` (int: months)
    - `image_url` (string)

  - Table: `inventory_items` (household instances)
    - `id` (PK)
    - `product_id` (FK -> product_master)
    - `household_id` (FK)
    - `location_id` (enum: Fridge, Freezer, Pantry)
    - `sub_location` (string: "Top Shelf", "Drawer 2")
    - `status` (enum: Raw, Cooked, Leftover, Open, Sealed)
    - `quantity` (float)
    - `unit` (string)
    - `entry_date` (timestamp)
    - `expiry_date` (timestamp)
    - `is_frozen` (boolean)

  - Table: `leftover_metadata`
    - `inventory_id` (FK -> inventory_items)
    - `original_recipe_id` (nullable)
    - `photo_url` (nullable)
    - `servings_remaining` (int)

2) MoveToFreezer / Freezer Bridge (server-side logic)
  - Pseudocode:
    FUNCTION MoveToFreezer(itemID):
      item = GetItem(itemID)
      product = GetProduct(item.product_id)
      new_expiry = now + product.default_freezer_life (months -> add months)
      UPDATE inventory_items SET location_id='Freezer', expiry_date=new_expiry, is_frozen=true, status='Cooked' WHERE id=itemID
      RETURN success, new_expiry

3) Smart Defrost Logic (Defrost Penalty)
  - When moving from Freezer -> Fridge (defrost):
    - Default new expiry = now + 48 hours (2 days) for thawed items (configurable per category)
    - Optionally prompt: "Cooking today? Move to Meal Plan?"

4) Safety Persona & Grace Periods (onboarding + learning)
  - Onboarding: quick "Vibe Check" with 2-second scenario slides to classify user as Purist/Pragmatist/Adventurer.
  - Personas map to notification & grace-period behavior:
    - Purist: strict, alert 24h before expiry
    - Pragmatist: friendly, alert on date
    - Adventurer: relaxed, alert 48h after date
  - Post-mortem learning: when user marks an expired item as [I Ate It] or [Tossed It], update per-user adjustments for that category (increase or decrease grace window).

5) UI: Gray Zone & Non-Perishable Exceptions
  - Gray Zone: items in a grace period should be dimmed/gray (not full red) and grouped separately to avoid notification fatigue.
  - Non-Perishable list: treat salt/honey/vinegar as infinite, no expiry notifications.

isk & Immortals Summary

Absolute Immortals: honey, salt, sugar, distilled white vinegar, cornstarch, pure vanilla extract, white rice — treat as no-expiry; UI: show ∞ / "Shelf Stable".
Long-Haulers (10+ yrs): dried beans/lentils, pure maple syrup, soy sauce, bouillon, instant coffee — treat as staple unless user marks low.
Anti-Immortals: brown rice, whole wheat flour, spices — do not mark immortal; prefer "Refresh" suggestions.
Danger Zone Top 10 (hard guards)

Ground meats, fresh poultry, leafy greens (bagged), raw shellfish, deli meats (opened), soft cheeses (opened), sprouts, leftover rice, raw eggs (cracked), pre-cut melons.
Behavior: zero grace period for some (ground meats: max 2 days fridge), forced actions for others (leftover rice hard 4-day cap).
Fried Rice Syndrome

If a user logs Cooked Rice (or type: 'leftover' + name matches "rice"), enforce hard cap = 4 days since capture; notification copy: "Cooked Rice alert! 🍚 It’s been 4 days. Even if it looks fine, Bacillus cereus isn't worth the risk. Time to toss."
Risk Levels / Product Master

Add columns on product_master:
is_immortal: boolean
risk_level: number (1–5)
Semantics:
Level 5: High-risk (ground beef, raw fish) — urgent, daily loud notifications; no grace.
Level 4: Perishables (milk, cooked meals) — strong suggestions (cook/freeze).
Level 3: Produce — waste-conscious suggestions.
Level 2: Hardy fridge — casual check-ins.
Level 1: Staples — inventory reminders only.
